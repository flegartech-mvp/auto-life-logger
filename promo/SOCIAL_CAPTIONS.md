## LinkedIn (2-3 short paragraphs, professional but not dry)

I wanted to know where my browser time was actually going — not a rough guess, but precise session data, classified by whether the site was productive or not. So I built a Chrome extension that does exactly that.

AutoLifeLogger tracks active tab sessions locally, classifies every domain as productive, distracting, or neutral, and calculates a daily discipline score from 0-100. It also detects behavioural patterns I didn't know I had — distraction loops, time overruns, and micro-distractions that fragment focus. Strict Mode can block distracting sites outright. Everything runs in chrome.storage.local; nothing is sent anywhere.

It's a Chrome MV3 extension, pure vanilla JS, and it's free. If you spend most of your working day in a browser and want honest data about your focus, the repo is public.
